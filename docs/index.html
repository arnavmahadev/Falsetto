A from-scratch reimplementation of the Segment Transformer — telling AI-generated music from human recordings by the structure of the piece.

Reproducing Kim & Go — Segment Transformer (arXiv:2509.08283) & Fusion Segment Transformer (arXiv:2601.13647) · Code

Architecture complete 56 tests passing Stage-1 trained · 0.97 AUC (proxy)

Abstract

AI music generators have gotten good enough that a single clip can fool a listener. The insight of the Segment Transformer is that a whole track gives itself away in its structure — how its segments repeat, echo, and relate over time.

FALSETTO reimplements the two papers end-to-end: the four segment encoders (MERT, Wav2Vec 2.0, Music2vec, and the FX effect-encoder), the two-stage detector (a short-audio segment classifier feeding a full-track Segment Transformer over a self-similarity matrix), and the Fusion model's gated bi-directional cross-attention — plus the data pipeline, training, evaluation, inference, CLI, and tests. The architecture is complete and unit-tested, and we've trained Stage-1 end-to-end on a proxy task (real music vs. MusicGen — see below); reproducing the paper's full benchmark numbers is a scale-up that needs the access-gated, full-scale datasets. This page shares that honestly — the published results we reproduce, our own functional check, and what's still to measure.

Method

Detection runs in two stages, matched to the papers' design.

Stage 1 — segment

Short-audio classifier

A pretrained encoder embeds a few-second clip; a light head calls it real or generated. MERT leads among the encoders.

clipMERTheadreal / AI
Stage 2 — structure

Segment Transformer

Beat-tracked 4-bar segments become an embedding sequence; a self-similarity matrix exposes repetition, and a dual-pathway transformer reads content and structure together. Fusion adds a gated cross-attention layer to balance the two.

Our result — Stage-1 functional check

We trained Stage-1 (frozen MERT → AudioCAT) end-to-end on a free Colab GPU and measured it on a held-out test split. This is a proxy task — real recordings vs. MusicGen, 120 clips per class — so read it as a demonstration that the pipeline trains and separates real from AI-generated audio, not a reproduction of the paper's benchmark (which needs the access-gated, full-scale SONICS / FakeMusicCaps data).
